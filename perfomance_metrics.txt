# 📊 Performance Metrics
## 1\. Accuracy
- Measures how correctly the system detects drowsiness.
- Formula:

    Accuracy=Correct PredictionsTotal PredictionsAccuracy = \\frac{Correct\\ Predictions}{Total\\ Predictions}Accuracy=Total PredictionsCorrect Predictions​
- Expected: **90–95%** (depending on lighting and face visibility)
---
## 2\. Precision
- Out of all detected drowsy events, how many were actually correct.
- Formula:

    Precision=True PositivesTrue Positives+False PositivesPrecision = \\frac{True\\ Positives}{True\\ Positives + False\\ Positives}Precision=True Positives+False PositivesTrue Positives​
- High precision → fewer false alarms
---
## 3\. Recall (Sensitivity)
- Ability to detect actual drowsy cases.
- Formula:

    Recall=True PositivesTrue Positives+False NegativesRecall = \\frac{True\\ Positives}{True\\ Positives + False\\ Negatives}Recall=True Positives+False NegativesTrue Positives​
- High recall → system rarely misses drowsiness
---
## 4\. F1-Score
- Balance between precision and recall.
- Formula:

    F1=2×Precision×RecallPrecision+RecallF1 = 2 \\times \\frac{Precision \\times Recall}{Precision + Recall}F1=2×Precision+RecallPrecision×Recall​
---
## 5\. False Positive Rate (FPR)
- When system wrongly detects drowsiness.
- Example: driver is alert but alarm triggers.
---
## 6\. False Negative Rate (FNR)
- When system fails to detect drowsiness.
- Critical metric (should be as low as possible)
---
## 7\. Eye Aspect Ratio (EAR) Threshold Performance
- Measures effectiveness of EAR threshold (e.g., 0.25)
- Metrics:
    - Average EAR (awake vs drowsy)
    - Blink detection accuracy
---
## 8\. Detection Time / Response Time
- Time taken to trigger alert after detecting drowsiness
- Expected: **< 2 seconds**
---
## 9\. Frame Processing Rate (FPS)
- Number of frames processed per second
- Expected:
    - 15–30 FPS for real-time performance
---
## 10\. System Latency
- Delay between camera capture and alert output
- Includes:
    - Image processing delay
    - Serial communication delay (Arduino)
---
## 11\. Robustness
- Performance under different conditions:
    - Low light 🌙
    - Glasses 😎
    - Head tilt 🤕
- Can be evaluated qualitatively or via accuracy drop %
---
## 12\. Alert Effectiveness
- Measures how quickly and reliably alerts are triggered:
    - Buzzer activation ✔
    - Telegram notification ✔
    - Arduino response ✔
---
